# Basic_FMCG_Dashboard
**# 🛒 FMCG Sales & Performance Dashboard

> Track key sales, profit, and operational metrics across regions, categories, and channels — all in one place.

---

## What This Dashboard Does

This dashboard gives you a bird's-eye view of your FMCG (Fast-Moving Consumer Goods) business performance. Whether you're a sales manager checking monthly targets, a category analyst digging into product trends, or an executive reviewing regional health — this is your single source of truth.

It covers roughly two years of data (May 2023 – April 2025) and lets you slice everything by region, category, product, sales channel, and customer segment.

## What's Inside

### 📈 Sales & Profit Trend
A dual-line chart showing monthly Net Sales and Profit over time. Useful for spotting seasonal dips, growth momentum, or anything that looks off.

### 🗂️ Sales by Category
A horizontal bar chart breaking down sales across five main categories:

- **Household** — 7.3M
- **Snacks** — 7.3M
- **Beverages** — 7.2M
- **Personal Care** — 7M
- **Dairy** — 6.9M

All five are performing within a tight range, which means no single category is carrying the business — and none is being left behind.

### 🗺️ Region by Sales
An interactive map of Bangladesh showing sales intensity by division. Darker shading means higher sales volume. Great for identifying which territories need more attention.

### 🏆 Top 10 Products by Sales
The ten best-performing products, led by:

1. Energy Drink — 985.5K
2. Soap — 973.6K
3. Dishwash Liquid — 967.7K
4. Biscuits — 952.2K
5. Chocolate — 946.9K

...down to Chips at 899.9K. Notably close in performance — no runaway winner, which suggests a healthy and balanced product portfolio.

### 🛍️ Sales by Channel
A donut chart showing how sales are distributed across:

- **Grocery Store** — 44.3% (dominant channel)
- **Super Shop** — 25.8%
- **Online** — 15.4%
- **Wholesale** — 14.5%

The grocery store channel leads by a wide margin, but online is a meaningful and growing slice worth watching.

### 👥 Sales by Customer Segment
A stacked bar chart comparing three customer types — **Regular**, **Budget**, and **Premium** — across six divisions: Chattogram, Barishal, Khulna, Rajshahi, Sylhet, and Dhaka.

Regular customers make up the bulk of volume across the board. Premium customers are a smaller but potentially higher-value segment worth nurturing.

### 📊 Profit Margin by Category
A bar chart showing profit margin per category, with a green reference line at the average margin of **15.45%**. Household and Beverages are top performers; Dairy sits slightly below average.

### 🔵 Profit Distribution Bubble Chart
A scatter plot where each bubble represents a category. Bubble size = quantity sold, X-axis = profit, Y-axis = net sales. It tells you at a glance which categories are high-volume, high-profit, or doing one without the other.

## Filters Available

You can narrow down any chart or metric using these filters in the left panel:

| Filter | Options |
|---|---|
| Date Range | Custom date picker (default: May 2023 – Apr 2025) |
| Region | Filter by division/region |
| Category | Household, Snacks, Beverages, Personal Care, Dairy |
| Product | Individual SKU-level filtering |
| Sales Channel | Grocery Store, Super Shop, Online, Wholesale |
| Customer Segment | Regular, Budget, Premium |

All filters work together — selecting a region will update every chart on the page simultaneously.

---

## How to Use It

1. **Start with the KPI cards** at the top to get a feel for the overall health of the business.
2. **Use the date filter** to compare specific periods (e.g., last quarter vs. the same quarter last year).
3. **Pick a region or category** to zoom in — all the charts below will update to reflect your selection.
4. **Check the trend line** to see if you're on the right trajectory within your filtered view.
5. **Cross-reference channels and segments** to understand *who* is buying *what* and *where*.
